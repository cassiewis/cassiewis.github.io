GFU Live

View Project on GitHub

This project is based off the desire to know how busy study areas are around a college campus. The goal of the project was to create a user friendly website to show accurate readings of how many bluetooth devices are currently in a general area or building. This would present the user with knowledge of how busy a building was without having to be there.

The Vitibot is a rover that can autonomously traverse a vineyard, gathering image data to predict grape yield months before harvest with impressive accuracy. The project has achieved proof of concept, and the team is currently making upgrades to the autonomous navigation capabilities and data cleanup used to further increase prediction accuracy. Dynamic Environment Audio Feedback

Assistive Technology Device for the Deaf and HH

Machine Learning, Sound Wave Analysis, Python

Dynamic Environment Audio Feedback

Servant Engineering

Our objective for this project was to enhance the perception of individuals with hearing impairments by developing an assistive device that facilitates directional hearing.

The physical design of our device resembles backpack straps, incorporating directional microphones and haptic feedback mechanisms. Through the utilization of a machine learning model, the sounds captured by the microphone are classified as either alert or non-alert, providing the user with notifications through the actuator. To facilitate this functionality, we compiled a sound corpus to store our data. We organized the files by implementing a Python script and trained our machine learning model on this data to accurately categorize different sounds. The effectiveness of the final model can be observed in the form of a confusion matrix, showcasing its accuracy, and visual representations of the sound waves that the model distinguishes.

While the physical end product was not developed as part of our project, we successfully completed the software component, which has been meticulously optimized to deliver a highly efficient and effective solution.

Tsunami Prediction

A Machine Learning Model's Prediction of Tsunami Occurances

SVM, Cross-Validation, Data Visualization, Python, Plotting

Tsunami Prediction

Data Science
View Project on GitLab

This course project involved selecting a specific domain and applying cross-validation techniques to train and test an SVM-based classification model. I utilized a database of earthquake data from around the world to train the model and make predictions regarding the occurrence of tsunamis.

To evaluate the model's performance, I computed both raw and balanced accuracy scores. Based on these results, I made necessary adjustments to improve the accuracy of the model. I visualized the outcomes through various plots, including a map plot displayed below, which highlighted the locations of the correct predictions made by the model. Additionally, I created a confusion matrix to gain insights into areas where the model exhibited inaccuracies.

I presented my findings and results, providing an overview of the model's performance. Within the presentation, I discussed potential improvements for future implementations, such as incorporating coastline data during training. Considering that the model inaccurately predicted tsunamis occurring on islands or in distant inland regions, incorporating the model's knowledge of the distance to the coastline could enhance its decision-making capabilities.

Capitol Incident Response Plan

Computer Security & Digital Forensics

In response to the 2021 Capitol Breach, our team took on the task of generating a comprehensive recovery plan for a Cyber Security Response Team. Drawing upon the "6 Step Incident Response Plan Outline," we meticulously crafted a set of steps and procedures aimed at not only recovering lost information but also mitigating the risk of future attacks. Our plan encompassed a systematic approach to identify and assess the impact of the breach, contain the incident, eradicate any malicious presence, recover data and systems, investigate the incident, and implement measures to prevent similar breaches in the future.
